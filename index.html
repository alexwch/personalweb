I am Chong Wang (Chinese name: 王翀).
I am an assistant professor in information systems in the College of Business at the City University of Hong Kong. I got my PhD in Business Administration (Information Systems) from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (supervisor: Professor Michael Zhang ). My research focus on understanding the social and economic impacts of information technologies.

Please feel free to browse the site, or send me an email if you would like to contact me.

Research

I am interested in the social and economic impacts of modern information technology.

My research primarily focuses on:

  • the impacts of online social network on users (participation, adoption, privacy, prosocial behavior)
  • the structure and evolution of online social networks
  • collective intelligence and crowd sourcing
  • online marketplaces (online labor market, E-commerce platforms, etc.)
  • Internet and financial innovation

I approach my research questions with multiple research methods, including formal analytical model, classical econometric analysis, social network analysis, and Bayesian structural modelling. Each methodology brings a unique angle and advantage in theory development.
